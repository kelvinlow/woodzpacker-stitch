01 Foundations

The Origin of Essence | 香之本源

In the silent depths of Southeast Asian rainforests, a transformation occurs that defies conventional logic. Agarwood, often referred to as "Oud" or "King of Incense," is not a species of tree but a result of a biological defense mechanism. It is the fragrant heart of the Aquilaria tree, formed only when it is wounded—by wind, lighting, or the passage of insects.

沉香并非树种之名，而是瑞香科植物在受到外界伤害后，为了自我修复而分泌的树脂，经百年沉淀而成。

Identifying Quality | 品级之辨

True Zenith Agarwood is defined by its density and resin content. To the seasoned connoisseur, quality is revealed through the 'Four Senses': Visual, Olfactory, Tactile, and Spiritual. High-grade agarwood will sink in water—a physical manifestation of the sheer density of its resinous concentration.

Sinkability (Chen)

The ultimate indicator of resin density. Only the rarest pieces possess the weight to settle beneath the surface.

Resin Pattern

Irregular black and brown veins that weave through the wood like celestial ink strokes.

"Incense is the bridge between the material world and the meditative void."

The Olfactory Profile | 气味之魂

The scent of Zenith Agarwood is impossible to fully describe, for it evolves with the warmth of the skin or the embers of the coal. It begins with a cool, medicinal clarity, shifting gradually into notes of sun-dried honey, ancient earth, and a lingering sweetness that remains in the room long after the smoke has dissipated.

上等沉香的气息具有层次感：先是清凉的药香，继而散发出蜜甜与泥土的芬芳，余味悠长。
